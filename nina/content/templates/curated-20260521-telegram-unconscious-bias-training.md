Unconscious bias training: does it work, or is it corporate theatre?

Short answer: only if done properly.

Most organisations run a half-day workshop, everyone nods along, and six months later nothing's changed. That's not training. That's ticking a box.

Here's what actually moves the needle:

1. Specificity over generalisation. "We all have biases" is true and useless. Train for your actual decisions: hiring, promotions, project allocations.

2. Accountability mechanisms. Bias training without structural changes is a TED Talk with sandwiches. Who reviews decisions? What's the process when bias is flagged?

3. Repetition, not revelation. One session doesn't rewire brains. Spaced practice, real scenarios, feedback loops.

4. Measure something. If you can't show behaviour change, you're running an awareness campaign, not training.

The research backs this. Brief diversity training often backfires or fizzles. Extended, integrated programmes with organisational buy-in? Different story.
