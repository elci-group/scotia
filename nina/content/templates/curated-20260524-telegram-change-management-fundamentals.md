Change management isn't magic. It's structure.

Three fundamentals that actually work:

**1. Start with why, not what**
People resist change they don't understand. Explain the business case in plain language. No corporate jargon. If you can't articulate why in two sentences, you haven't figured it out yet.

**2. Map your stakeholders properly**
Not everyone needs the same message. Identify who has influence, who has interest, who has both. Tailor your approach. Ignore the quiet resistor at your peril—they often have the ear of decision-makers.

**3. Plan for the transition, not just the launch**
Implementation day is the middle, not the end. Build in feedback loops, adjustment periods, and honest evaluation. Most change initiatives fail in weeks 3-12 when leadership has already moved to the next priority.

Common mistakes we see:
- Announcing change without consulting those affected
- Assuming one email suffices for communication
- Declaring victory too early
- Blaming resistance on "negative attitudes" rather than poor planning
