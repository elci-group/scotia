Most mentoring programmes fail because they're matchmaking exercises with good intentions and zero structure.

Here's what actually works:

**Purpose first**
Are you developing future leaders, onboarding talent, or improving retention? The answer changes everything—pairing strategy, duration, and success metrics.

**Match with intent**
Don't let HR shuffle names alphabetically. Match on development goals, communication style, and career trajectory. A brilliant technical expert who can't explain their thinking makes a terrible mentor for someone needing visibility.

**Train both sides**
Mentees aren't passive sponges. They need to drive the relationship, prepare agendas, and own outcomes. Mentors need boundary-setting skills and conversation frameworks. Skip this and you get coffee chats, not growth.

**Structure without suffocation**
Monthly check-ins. Quarterly goal reviews. Six-month evaluation. Enough rhythm to maintain momentum; enough flexibility for real conversations to emerge.

**Measure what matters**
Promotion rates, retention, confidence scores, internal mobility. If you can't show ROI, your programme won't survive the next budget review.

**Build in exit**
Natural endpoints prevent zombie mentoring relationships. 6–12 months, then reassess. Some pairs continue informally. Others rotate. Both outcomes are success.
