Remote teams don't fail because of distance. They fail because of ambiguity.

Here's what actually works:

**Over-communicate on purpose**
No one walks past your desk anymore. Document decisions. Share context. Repeat yourself in writing.

**Kill the meeting bloat**
Daily stand-ups? Fine. Six back-to-back video calls? Burnout. Async updates beat performative attendance.

**Set output standards, not availability standards**
Replying in 3 minutes proves nothing. Shipping quality work on deadline proves everything.

**Invest in real relationships**
Remote ≠ disconnected. Structured 1:1s. Occasional in-person gatherings. Know your people as humans.

**Equip them properly**
Slow laptops and VPN nightmares signal you don't care. Fix the basics.

**Watch for isolation signals**
Performance drops, camera-always-off, missed check-ins. These are data points, not personality quirks.
