Culture transformation isn't a poster campaign. It's a operational overhaul with casualties.

Most "culture change" fails because leadership wants new vibes without new systems. You cannot train your way out of a toxic structure.

What actually works:

🔹 Audit before announcing. Map decision-making, promotion patterns, who gets heard in rooms. Data first, slogans later.

🔹 Fire the blockers. One senior person actively undermining change outperforms ten workshops. HR knows who. Act.

🔹 Redesign incentives. Behaviours follow rewards. If collaboration is the goal, stop exclusively rewarding individual billings.

🔹 Middle managers are your battlefield. They translate strategy to daily reality. Underinvest here and watch initiative die in translation.

🔹 Measure what matters. Engagement surveys are lagging indicators. Track decision speed, internal referral rates, exit interview themes by department.

Timeline reality: surface shifts in 6 months. Embedded change? 18-24 minimum. Anyone promising faster is selling theatre.
