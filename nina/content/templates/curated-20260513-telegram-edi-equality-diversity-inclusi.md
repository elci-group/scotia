EDI training isn't a checkbox. It's a business tool.

Most companies get this wrong. They book a half-day seminar, hand out rainbow lanyards, and wonder why nothing changes. Six months later, the same grievances land on HR's desk.

Here's what actually works:

**Investigate first.** Before training anyone, know where your problems are. Exit interviews, grievance data, pay audits. Train blind and you're throwing money away.

**Link to policy.** EDI training must connect to your disciplinary framework, recruitment process, and promotion criteria. Otherwise it's just nice words with no teeth.

**Make it practical.** Tribunal cases don't fail because someone missed the theory. They fail because a manager didn't know how to handle a disclosure, or a recruitment panel asked illegal questions. Train for the situations your people actually face.

**Follow through.** One session changes nothing. Embed it in mentoring, team building, and leadership development. Measure behaviour change, not attendance sheets.
