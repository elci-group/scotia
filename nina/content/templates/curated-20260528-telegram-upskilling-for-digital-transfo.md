**What actually works:**

**Audit first.** Map current skills against where your business is heading in 3-5 years. Not generic "digital skills"—specific capabilities your strategy demands.

**Make it practical.** No death by PowerPoint. Real projects, real tools, real outcomes. People learn by doing, not watching.

**Embed in workflow.** Separate "training days" fail. Micro-learning, peer mentoring, and stretch assignments build habits.

**Measure obsessively.** Did productivity improve? Error rates drop? Retention rise? If you can't prove ROI, you're guessing.

**Lead from the top.** Managers who don't upskill themselves signal it's optional. It isn't.

Common mistake: chasing every new platform. Focus on adaptive mindsets and core digital literacy over tool-specific training. Tools change. Learning agility doesn't.
