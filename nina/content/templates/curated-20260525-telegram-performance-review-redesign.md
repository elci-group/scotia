Performance reviews are broken. Annual box-ticking exercises that everyone dreads and nobody trusts. Time to redesign.

Here's what actually works:

**Kill the rating scale**
Numbers demotivate. Conversations develop. Swap 1-5 grids for structured dialogue.

**Frequency beats formality**
Quarterly check-ins, not yearly surprises. Problems fester for 11 months when you wait.

**Peer input, not peer ranking**
360-degree feedback helps. Stack ranking destroys teams. Know the difference.

**Employee owns the narrative**
Let them lead. Manager listens, coaches, aligns. Power imbalance shrinks; honesty grows.

**Link to development, not just distribution**
If reviews only determine pay, you've built a compensation tool disguised as growth. Separate the conversations or clarify the connection.

**Train your managers**
Most flounder because nobody taught them how. Redesign the process without redesigning the people running it? Waste of time.

**Document lightly**
Bureaucracy kills engagement. Capture agreements, not novels.
