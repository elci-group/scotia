Employee engagement metrics: stop measuring vanity, start measuring what matters.

Most companies track participation rates in surveys and call it engagement. It's not.

Three metrics that actually predict performance:

**Intent to stay.** Not satisfaction—commitment. Ask: "Would you recommend this workplace to a friend?" Then track who leaves within 12 months. Gap between answer and action = trust problem.

**Discretionary effort.** What do people do above job description? Track volunteer rates for projects, peer mentoring uptake, process improvements submitted. High engagement shows up in behaviours, not scores.

**Speed of internal mobility.** Engaged employees develop. If your high performers sit in roles 4+ years with no growth, your metrics are lying to you. Measure promotion velocity and lateral move rates.

What to drop:
- eNPS without follow-up (lazy data)
- Training hours completed (attendance ≠ learning)
- Pulse survey frequency (more surveys, less honesty)

Red flag: engagement scores rise while productivity and retention flatline. Your survey is broken, not your people.
