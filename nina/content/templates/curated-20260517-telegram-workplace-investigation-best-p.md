Workplace investigations go wrong when organisations wing it. Here's how to do it properly.

**Act fast, but not recklessly**
Delay destroys evidence and erodes trust. But rushing without a plan creates procedural holes that tank outcomes.

**Pick the right investigator**
Internal for minor issues. External when senior staff are involved, or when independence matters legally. Conflict of interest sinks credibility.

**Document everything**
Notes. Timestamps. Versions. Assume it'll be scrutinised by a tribunal. Because it might be.

**Follow your own policies**
Nothing undermines a fair process faster than ignoring the rules you wrote. Review them first.

**Preserve confidentiality**
Leakage poisons workplace culture and exposes you to claims. Need-to-know only.

**Give respondents clear allegations**
Vague accusations = unfair process = legal risk. Specifics, timelines, evidence.

**Actually listen to witnesses**
Leading questions corrupt findings. Open questions, exact quotes, no editorialising.

**Make decisions on evidence, not instinct**
"Gut feel" doesn't hold up. Balance of probabilities. Document your reasoning.

**Communicate outcomes properly**
Respondent, complainant, relevant managers. Appropriate detail, appropriate channels.

**Learn from it**
Investigation reports gather dust. Use findings to fix systemic gaps.
