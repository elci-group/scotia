AI in L&D: hype or actual utility?

It's both. Here's the breakdown.

**What's working now**
- Personalised learning paths at scale
- Instant content generation (first drafts, not final products)
- Data on skill gaps you can actually use
- 24/7 coaching bots for basic queries

**What's still broken**
- Context awareness. AI doesn't know your office politics, your failing product line, or why your sales team actually missed target.
- Soft skills development. Try teaching empathy via algorithm. Good luck.
- Compliance nuance. Regulatory interpretation needs human judgement.

**The real risk**
L&D teams outsourcing thinking to tools. AI writes your policy, designs your workshop, measures "engagement." You stop asking whether the learning worked. Spoiler: it probably didn't.

**The rule**
AI handles volume. Humans handle stakes.

Corporate events? AI can suggest venues. It can't read a room when the merger announcement lands wrong.
Mentoring? AI matches profiles. It doesn't replace the conversation where someone admits they're drowning.
