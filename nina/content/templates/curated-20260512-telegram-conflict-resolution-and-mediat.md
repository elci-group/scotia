Conflict costs money. Mediation saves it.

Most workplace disputes don't need lawyers. They need people who can listen, reframe, and negotiate.

Here's what actually works:

**1. Separate positions from interests**
"I want a transfer" (position) vs "I need to feel respected" (interest). Address the root, not the request.

**2. Control the room**
Set ground rules. Equal airtime. No interruptions. Neutral territory. Structure prevents escalation.

**3. Reframe, don't react**
"You're undermining me" becomes "There's a concern about communication boundaries." Language shapes outcomes.

**4. Build the BATNA**
Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement. Know your walk-away point before you start.

**5. Get agreement in writing**
Verbal commitments dissolve. Documented ones stick.
